Student Info

Since the inception of the cup in, it has developed in variation and design. The earliest mug discovered dated back to 10000 BCE and was carved from bone, however in 12,000 years, it has still retained its basic function but has furthered in available materials and design methods in which can be implemented. I have decided to analyse and critique an imitation of an ornate Hitkari (est. 1947) Bone China mug which was found in my own home. With this, I will discuss the original intentions of this specific product, its general aesthetics/appeal of its design and how impactful or effective these overall design choices are.

The Aesthetic

The mug sports two floral designs (one on each opposing side of the mug) representing a pink rose bunch, most common in European countries, often known as the Rosa ‘Bella’ meaning “beautiful rose” in Italian. Both “beautiful rose” design depicts the blooming process of the flower, with each side’s focal points being 3 main flowers in varying stages of blooming, accompanied with several rosebuds in the surrounding.

With these floral designs contrasting with the natural colour of the bone China, a decorative gold trim has been utilized on both the lip and base of the vintage mug in order to compliment both the base white and pink rose artwork. Gold and white are a very common combination of colours which often represents royalty or wealth. The muted saturation and colours that have been implemented into both artworks contrast well with the pale bone background, however the strong green inappropriately becomes the main emphasis of the print.

The Concept
The gold trim and bare bone coloured ceramic was a very common design for these variations of mugs developed by Hitkari Potteries. The only difference between sets and other series of mugs/china sets was that the floral design would vary (examples provided).

With this discovery after researching the product and its history, I am very doubtful that the Pink Rose design that was presented on the mug I provided was actually intended to compliment the basic shape and bare design. Rather, the designs’ purpose was most likely simply decorate the mug with considerations for ease of mass manufacturing and printing on to the (again) mass produced bone china mugs.

Contrary to my doubts though, shape of the mug with its bulbous design could be perceived as similar in shape to that of a flower blossom, in which case, the theming of the mug is quite impeccable. Nonetheless, even though the theming of product is very well thought out, the overall aesthetics just do not appear to be coherent in its elements.

The Context
Being made as a high end and expensive product, it was solely used for entertaining guests and family and rarely would be for personal use on occasion. In my analysis of the history of the product and the aesthetic utilized, I believe that this mug is targeted mainly to affluent individuals/families or to those who wish to appear as affluent or wealthy. As well as this, the floral artworks of the Rosa ‘Bella’ led me to believe this cup was also somewhat designed specifically to target a Europeans and most likely those of Italian heritage/culture groups. The history of this product in my home supports my theory, with it being gifted to my parents in 1990 as a housewarming gift by my Grandparents on my Fathers’ side, whom are both immigrants of Italy.

To conclude this critical analysis the product does not majorly follow many of modern design principles, however, without these principles it somehow produces a unique design of its own which is difficult for me to suggest improvements upon. From a Product Design perspective, the mug values its distinctive shape and aesthetic over its practicality. As well as this, the Image and Visual design are the same with its artwork and use of the colour of the bone porcelain.  It could almost be comparable to a piece of art at most.
