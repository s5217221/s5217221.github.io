Student Info

Throughout the first half of the Trimester, the first assessment required students to complete 4 tasks, each correlating with weekly tutorials where students were presented with the content and knowledge required to complete these tasks. Task 1 (interpreted to evoke creativity within students) required design students to create 7 arts pieces, preferable each being a variation of a given subject and each piece utilizing differing methods of creation. Task 2 (to induce observation and documentation) asked for students to examine a series of interactions that are commonly experienced every day. Task 3 (establishing research) required students to analyse the 3 design pillars of ontology and present examples that followed these pillars.


Week 1
Building creativity was the key take away from this given task. For this, I had decided to create 7 variations of a common red Rose. This proved to be one of the more difficult tasks when compared to the other 3, requiring the use of differing materials and creative thinking methods. After using methods from digital to physical (pen and paper and computational drawing), I turned to Artificial Intelligence. This presented an interesting avenue of exploration that I believed would assist me in the production of a unique piece. Although the final work produced by was not the most coherent, it definitely gave a valuable insight into the current stages of AI development and its potential for future generations to improve and implement.

Week 2
Utilising our powers of observation/analysis were expected to complete this task, requiring us to deconstruct the very things that we find commonplace interactions in our everyday lives. In this case, I decided to document the implementation of doors/doorways, examining their interaction methods and how they vary between environments and settings. It was very interesting to note that the general accessibility of a given door was dependant mainly on its location, and who/what required that accessibility (those of differing disabilities, guide animals, vehicles and so on).

Week 3

Alongside the first task, this task proved to be one of the more difficult. This demanded we interpret Arturo Escobar (pp.110-116) vision regarding Ontological design theory. With this, it was divided into its core 3 pillars. With each pillar, described ways of how ontology was implemented and essentially these examples deconstruction and examination gave an insight into how Ontology impacted our overall lives, and how it can continue to do so. However, its interpretation was awkward to decipher, as well as researching to provide an example to correlate for each of the 3 design pillars.

Week 4
A creative and critical analysis proved to be a powerful for future reference, not just in terms of analysing another individuals production, but our own as well. This type of scrutinization generally evoked me to become more self-critical of my own productions and works and overall allowed wide range of methods of self improvement.
